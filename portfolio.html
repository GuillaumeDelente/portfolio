About me

What keep my gears turning

Even though developing native Android Apps is my main area of expertise, I am able to work across all layers. I play with technologies such as Django and Ruby On Rails to design elegant APIs. I also have a growing interest in building scalable software, and have experience with Amazon EC2, Google App Engine and RightScale.

A little background

I was born and raised in the South West of France, surrounded by the ocean and vineyards. I used a keyboard for the first time in 1998, when my father bought the first family computer. A few years later, I taught myself HTML and began creating websites as a hobby. My mom was always yelling at me because nobody could reach us on the phone while I was using up all the time left on the 24 hours/month 56k voiceband modem plan. I decided to study Computer Science when one of my websites reached 10,000 monthly views, allowing me to make a significant amount of money for a 15 year-old kid.

Education

My diplomas

I will graduate early 2014 from ENSEIRB with a Master’s Degree in Computer Science, and am completing my final year of schooling abroad at the University of California, Santa Barbara. In 2010, I received my Bachelor’s Degree in Computer Science with highest honors from Université Bordeaux I.

My story

I have quite an uncommon educational path; I broke down barriers to get where I wanted to be. After high school, I spent two years at SUPINFO before I realized it wasn’t what I was expecting from an Engineering education. I then realigned my goals to join ENSEIRB, which is part of the prestigious French Grandes Écoles. To be considered for admission, I had to receive a diploma from Université Bordeaux I in the best condition possible. I seized the opportunity and was ranked third out of 110, paving my way to ENSEIRB. I then applied to be a part of the University of California Education Abroad Program, and was selected to study at UC Santa Barbara during the Fall 2012 and Winter 2013 quarters.
